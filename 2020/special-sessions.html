Special Sessions

ACM Multimedia Asia 2020 will include three Special Sessions on innovative and frontier topics in the field of multimedia computing and retrieval. Special session papers, which can be invited or submitted, are limited to 6-8 pages, and double-blind in submission. They will supplement the regular research papers and be included in the proceedings of MM Asia 2020.

This year the special sessions aim to solicit papers on multimedia computing related to user behaviors, urban applications, and knowledge-driven analysis and retrieval. The topics include but are not limited to:

SS1: User Behavior-aware Multimedia Computing

  • Recommendation on multimedia content
  • Multi-modal dialog system
  • Multi-modal user/group profiling
  • User behavior analysis on multimedia content
  • Multimedia content understanding with user behaviors

SS2: Multimedia Computing for Urban Data Analysis and Applications

  • Multi-source, multi-modality urban data fusing
  • Cross-device, cross-domain urban multimedia data correlation
  • Spatial-temporal urban multimedia data modeling
  • Urban, multimodal data mining from social media
  • Visual urban perception
  • High-level urban attributes (e.g., the crime rate, safety, and education) learning from urban multimodal data
  • Large-scale visual geo-localization and multi-modal geo-localizationon
  • Deep neural networks for urban data representation
  • Multimedia computing systems for urban data analysis, retrieval, and recommendation
  • Urban multimedia data interaction and visualization
  • Urban multimedia data collections, benchmarking, and performance evaluation
  • Other methods in multimedia computing for novel applications in urban computing

SS3: Knowledge-Driven Analysis and Retrieval on Multimedia

  • Knowledge-driven cross-modal retrieval
  • Cross-modal knowledge extraction
  • Cross-modal knowledge analysis
  • Knowledge-driven multimedia applications
  • Multimedia knowledge graph
  • Knowledge-driven multimedia indexing, annotation and summarization
  • Knowledge-driven multimedia recommendation

The deadline for special paper submissions is 30 Oct 2020.
